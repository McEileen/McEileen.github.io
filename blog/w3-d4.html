Third Week of RC

Day 4: Public Speaking is a lot like hot sauce

March 7, 2019

My first year out of college, I lived with a housemate, Katie, who put hot sauce on everything. Rice, toast, her morning apple, everything. I also started to eat more hot sauce while I was living with Katie, and I found that my tolerance for it increased faster than expected. After a few months, I, too, enjoyed hot sauce on my toast.

My second year out of college, I worked for the Affordable Care Act (ACA) as an in-person counselor, which meant that I enrolled clients in health insurance and did educational outreach on health reform. Outreach required me to regularly do public speaking, and I quickly became used to speaking in front of groups, including those who didn't always love the ACA. Sure, I sometimes got nervous, but it wasn't much of an issue.

It's been years since I lived with Katie, and since that time, my tolerance for hot sauce has decreased. Similarly, I no longer have a job that includes regular public speaking, and I do feel that my attitude about it has shifted. I presented on debugging dockerized applications this Thursday at Recurse, and I could tell that I was more nervous than I used to get during ACA presentations. However, I know that in the same way tolerance for spicy food can come back, ease with public speaking can come back. I appreciated the opportunity to practice public speaking with a five minute talk, and I plan to do more in the future.
