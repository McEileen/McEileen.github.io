Fifth Week of RC

Day 2: i3 SkillShare

March 19, 2019

When I switched from Mac OSX to Ubuntu in November 2018, I wanted to find a tool that would replace ShiftIt, my old favorite tool for window management in Mac OSX. Friends from Lucy Parsons Labs recommended i3, and I've been using it since.

Today at Recurse, a group of us got together to do an i3 skillshare for about an hour. We started by pulling up the keyboard map for i3 and demo-ing some example commands. I learned that when a roomful of recursers is instructing you to try out different commands for splitting the screen, one of the hardest parts is agreeing on the difference between a horizontal and vertical split.

We also tried out the floating mode, which I still like less than sticking with a tiled layout. However, I was excited to learn that if you put a window in floating mode, then right click on the floating window while holding down your mod key, you can resize the window. Thanks, recursers, for all the tiling manager TILs.
