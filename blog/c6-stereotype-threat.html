Stereotype Threat

How To Identify?

August 17, 2015

I did a lot of thinking on stereotype threat last January when I attended Write, Speak, Code, a day-long seminar that trains women to be thought leaders in tech. The seminar was partly inspired by The Op-Ed Project, which trains women to write op-eds and which I had also attended in Atlanta. I sure do love attending seminars!

We did various exercises during the seminar, including one time when we sat in a group and talked about fears we have about our coding abilities. For example, I mentioned my fear that I take too long on assignments or that I am a slow coder. It was a relief to see other people raise their hands and to understand that I am not the only one who feels this way. The main organizer explained that research has been done on African-American students and stereotype threat which shows that when members of a group under stereotype threat discuss their fears about confirming a stereotype, it can help them to perform better. (I want to note that I attended this seminar months ago and my memory is a little fuzzy. Don't quote me directly - the organizer probably expressed this thought much more eloquently!)

I like to think of myself as a highly self-aware person, and working for social change has shown me how our identities affect the way we act with each other socially and in work environments. Nevertheless, I don't think I am very good at telling when I am affected by stereotype threat. I do think there were a few times in my winter part-time programming class when I feared asking a question because I didn't want my male classmates to think that my confusion reflected on all female programmers' aptitudes. However, it's harder for me to tell how stereotype threat affects me in Phase 0 because so much of our work is solitary. I also have a harmful tendency to be hard on myself regardless of what field I am working in or how women are expected to perform in that area. It's hard for me to distinguish between stereotype threat and generalized anxiety about my abilities. I hope the difference becomes clearer to me as Dev Bootcamp proceeds.
