Second Week of RC

Day 2: I Made a Mistake

February 26, 2019

This Tuesday I worked on a new feature for OpenOversight that would allow users to filter officers by the year they were employed. I’d already begun work on this functionality last week, when I added the `last_employment_date` and `last_employment_details` feature to the officer model. This week, my goal is to add a drop-down selector for the year field to each department page.

While starting on my new pull request (PR), I realized that the previous PR was incomplete. True, I’d updated the `officer` model and the individual officer profile pages, but I hadn’t added the new fields to the “create an officer” form. A bulk upload of officers would have included the new information, but not any new officers created through the form. I also hadn’t updated the “edit” forms. D’oh!

When I realize that I’ve made a mistake, I try to figure out why I made it, and then I try to strategize about how to avoid repeating my mistake. I had thought that I was well-organized in my approach to this PR, as I started out by making a checklist of all the tasks I needed to do on both the front-end and back-end. However, I forgot to include the form work on my checklist.

I think I failed to catch my mistake sooner because I didn’t test the functionality of adding a new officer from the front end. I did test that an officer with `last_employment_date` and `last_employment_details` would have the proper information displayed, but I didn’t create such an officer through the web interface. In the future, I’m going to avoid similar mistakes by more thoroughly testing new functionality I add to the app.
