I did several things right in this question to my friend. I started out by succinctly explaining the challenge, and I then showed two solutions that I had attempted. I also expressed politeness and gratitude. I showed due diligence by telling Rene that I had tried to use .to_s to convert what include? was receiving into a string. I further demonstrated due diligence by telling I had tried other methods, like has_value and find.

However, I think the layout of my email was somewhat unclear. It would have flowed better if I pasted the errors immediately below the solutions that raised them.

Rene responded and confirmed that I was running into problems because the method include? only works on strings. He then helped me find the right answer. I was very grateful for his help.
